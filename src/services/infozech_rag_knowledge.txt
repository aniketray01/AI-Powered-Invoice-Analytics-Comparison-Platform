1 — PRODUCT OVERVIEW

 1.1 What is iBill Copilot?
iBill Copilot is an AI-powered web application that helps telecom professionals understand and audit complex tower invoices. It processes Excel or CSV invoice files, performs forensic analysis, detects billing anomalies, and answers follow-up questions via a conversational chatbot.

Four-step pipeline:
1. Upload invoice (CSV/Excel)
2. Extract and store structured data
3. AI generates summary and chat
4. Export report as PDF or Excel

1.2 Problem Being Solved
Telecom tower invoices are among the most complex financial documents in the industry. A single invoice contains 20–40 line items covering energy charges, passive infrastructure rent, fiber connectivity, diesel surcharges, maintenance, and regulatory levies — each governed by different MSA clauses and rate slabs.

Three core pain points:
- Understanding each invoice takes 2–4 hours of manual work per analyst
- Disputes take days to resolve (email back-and-forth, manual investigation)
- Regional operators excluded — invoices are English-only, inaccessible to Hindi-speaking operators

1.3 Target Users

| User Type | Pain Point Solved |
|---|---|
| Tower Billing Analysts | Verify invoice line items instantly without manual MSA cross-referencing |
| Telecom Tenants (Airtel, Jio, Vi) | Understand what they're paying for and why charges changed |
| Operations Managers | Track OPEX trends across multiple tower sites |
| New Billing Team Joiners | Learn billing terminology through the chatbot |
| Regional Hindi-Speaking Operators | Read invoice summaries fully in Hindi |

1.4 Business Value
- Invoice query resolution: hours → under 60 seconds
- Manual billing effort reduced by over 90% per invoice cycle
- New analyst onboarding time reduced by 60%
- Zero revenue leakage from missed escalation clauses




3 — INVOICE TERMINOLOGY (RAG Chunks)

3.1 AMOUNT_NONENERGY — Non-Energy / Rent Charge
What it is: The base lease or rent component of a telecom tower invoice. Represents the fixed monthly cost for the land or roof space where the tower stands.

Includes: Ground lease, IP license fees, space fees, passive infrastructure rent.

Characteristics: Largest and most stable component of any tower invoice. Governed by MSA escalation clauses.

3.2 AMOUNT_ELECTRICITY — Electricity / Grid Energy Charge
What it is: Cost for grid electricity used to power the tower and communication equipment 24/7.

EB = Electricity Board (grid power supply).

Zero value means: Site is off-grid (diesel only), or electricity is included elsewhere, or a different billing cycle applies.

3.3 AMOUNT_DIESEL — Diesel / Generator Charge
What it is: Diesel fuel cost for the backup generator (DG = Diesel Generator) during power outages.

High diesel charges indicate: Frequent power cuts in that region. Off-grid dependency.

Diesel vs electricity: Diesel is typically more expensive per unit than grid electricity.


3.4 AMENDMENT_AMOUNT — Billing Correction / Adjustment
What it is: Retroactive billing corrections applied to the invoice. Can be positive (additional charges) or negative (credits).

Common amendment types: Escalation corrections, FX remeasurement, back-billing corrections, rent-free period adjustments, contractual retro charges.

Risk signal: Amendment share above 3% of total payable requires investigation.

AMENDMENT_ITEM column describes what the amendment is for.

3.5 BACK_BILL / Back-Billing
What it is: Charges raised in the current invoice for a previous billing period — retroactive corrections.

Why it happens: Missed charges, under-billing, or corrections for a past month.

Risk: Distorts month-to-month OPEX visibility. 100% back-bill rate = entire invoice is retroactive.

Ledger check: Verify BACK_BILL_MONTH and BACK_BILL_DATE. Cross-reference with previous month's invoice.

3.6 RENT FREE PERIOD
What it is: Contractual provision where no rent is charged for a specified period at lease start — typically an incentive for new tenants.

Forensic anomaly: If LEASESTART_DATE is after current BILL_MONTH → site is being billed before lease starts → overpayment → credit note required.

3.7 FX RATE — Foreign Exchange Rate
What it is: Conversion rate from USD (base currency) to local currency used in billing calculations.


Why it matters: FX fluctuations between periods cause amendment amounts even when USD base charge is unchanged.

---

3.8 CUMULATIVE_ESCALATION
What it is: Compound annual escalation factor applied to the base lease rate. Represents how much the original contracted rent has grown since lease start.

Value of 1.0 = no escalation applied yet. Value of 1.15 = 15% cumulative growth.

Typical rate: 5–10% per year per MSA contract.



---

3.9 GRID_STATUS
What it is: Whether a tower site is powered by commercial grid electricity or off-grid diesel.

| Value | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Grid | Site draws power from electricity board (EB) |
| Off-Grid / DG | Site runs entirely on diesel generators |



---

 3.10 SITE_TYPE
Greenfield: Standalone tower structure built on vacant land. Taller masts, dedicated ground space, higher rent due to land acquisition costs.

Rooftop: Antenna installation on existing buildings. Smaller footprint, lower rent, more complex structural agreements.


---
3.11 MULTI_TENANCY_STATUS
What it is: How many telecom operators share a single tower.
Multiple = more than one tenant (cost-shared infrastructure). Single = one operator only.


---

3.12 SITERRA_SITE_ID
What it is: Unique identifier for each tower site in the Siterra tower management system.

Ghana pattern: GHGR0440 (GH = Ghana, GR = Greater Accra region code, 0440 = sequential number)

Primary key used to reconcile sites across multiple invoices and track month-over-month changes.

---

3.13 REGION_NAME
What it is: Geographic region where the tower site is located.
---


 3.14 ON_AIR_PRORATED_FACTOR
What it is: Fraction of the month a site was active, used to calculate partial-month charges.
---


 3.15 AMD_BASE
What it is: Basis for calculating the amendment amount.

Energy Base = amendment based on energy charges (volatile — diesel prices, FX on electricity).
Non-Energy Base = amendment based on rent/lease charges (more stable — escalation, renegotiation).

---

 SECTION 4 — FORENSIC METRICS

 4.1 Back-Bill Rate
Formula: (back-bill records / total records) × 100%
Risk threshold: Above 5% = forensic risk signal


 4.2 Rent-Free Anomaly
Definition: LEASESTART_DATE is after current BILL_MONTH → overpayment
Action: Identify site, calculate overbilled amount, raise credit note


 4.3 Amendment Share
Formula: (|total amendment| / grand total) × 100%
Risk threshold: Above 3% = investigation required


 4.4 Off-Grid Rate
Formula: (off-grid records / total records) × 100%
Risk threshold: Above 20% = OPEX risk (volatile diesel prices)


 4.5 Multi-Amendment Sites
Definition: Sites with more than one amendment in the same invoice period
Risk: Double-billing signal

CHUNK 017 | TOPIC: Tower Industry Context & Infozech's Role
SOURCE: https://infozech.com/solutions/ and blogs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Tower Infrastructure Industry:
Tower companies (also called TowerCos) own and operate the passive
infrastructure (towers, masts, rooftops) on which telecom operators mount
their active equipment (antennas, radios).

Key tower companies globally include American Tower, Crown Castle, SBA
Communications, Indus Towers (India), ATC India, and others.

Infozech's target customers are Tower Companies and Telecom Operators (CSPs).

Key challenges in the tower industry that Infozech addresses:
- Billing complexity: Tower companies bill multiple tenants (operators)
  for sharing a single tower. Each operator may have different agreements,
  different energy consumption rates, and different service levels.
- Energy costs: Energy (diesel, grid electricity) is the single largest
  OPEX item for tower companies, often 30-50% of total costs.
- Asset management: Tower companies manage thousands to hundreds of
  thousands of distributed sites that need constant monitoring and
  maintenance.
- Compliance and auditability: Billing disputes between tower companies
  and operators are common; audit trails and transparent billing are
  critical.
- 5G rollout: New 5G infrastructure requirements are driving tower companies
  to manage more complex, denser site networks.

Infozech's AI initiatives:
Per their 2025 blog, Infozech has invested in AI that works in the field —
specifically for tower operations, field inspection, and operational
decision-making. They emphasize data quality as the foundation for
AI-driven operations.

CHUNK 018 | TOPIC: Billing Concepts Relevant to Infozech Products
SOURCE: Infozech domain knowledge
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Key billing concepts in the context of Infozech's iBill product:

MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT (MSA):
A legal contract between a tower company and a telecom operator (tenant)
that defines the terms of tower sharing, including rent, energy charges,
SLAs, and billing terms. iBill manages billing according to the MSA terms.

ENERGY BILLING IN TOWERS:
Tower companies supply diesel generators (DG) and/or grid electricity to
power operator equipment mounted on their towers. The energy cost is
separately billed to each tenant (operator) based on their actual consumption
or an agreed formula. iBill automates this energy billing.

IP BILLING:
IP (Internet Protocol) billing refers to billing for IP connectivity
services — such as backhaul bandwidth — provided by the tower company or
telecom operator. iBill handles both energy billing and IP billing in a
single platform.

BILLING CYCLE:
The periodic process (typically monthly) of calculating charges, generating
invoices, obtaining approvals, and collecting payment from operators.
iBill significantly reduces billing cycle times through automation.

REVENUE ASSURANCE:
The process of ensuring that all billable events are captured and billed
accurately, and that revenue leakage (unbilled or under-billed services)
is minimized. iBill includes built-in revenue assurance validation.

BILLING DISPUTE MANAGEMENT:
When operators disagree with charges raised by the tower company, a
formal dispute process is initiated. iBill provides the data trails and
reports needed to resolve disputes quickly.

INTRA CIRCLE ROAMING SETTLEMENT:
When telecom operators share network resources in the same geographic
circle, usage data must be tracked, reconciled, and settled financially
between operators. Infozech's iRecon product handles this.
